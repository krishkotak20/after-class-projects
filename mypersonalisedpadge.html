krish

I live my life like every day is a warm‑up for a match that may or may not exist. Football is the one thing that always makes sense to me, even when the rest of my brain is busy wondering why toast always lands butter‑side down or whether clouds ever get tired of floating. Sometimes I catch myself imagining ridiculous things, like coaching a team of hyper‑motivated garden gnomes or inventing a new celebration that involves interpretive dance. It’s chaotic, but it’s my kind of chaotic — football at the centre, and a whole universe of weird thoughts orbiting around it.

my interests

I’ve collected a strange mix of interests over the years, and honestly, half of them make no sense even to me. One day I’m fascinated by how jellyfish glow, the next I’m convinced I should learn how to carve tiny spoons out of soap. I’ll happily spend an hour watching videos of people restoring rusty tools, then suddenly switch to researching why ducks walk like they’re late for something important. I get weirdly invested in things like ranking the best shapes of pasta or imagining what my life would be like if I owned a pet llama. My brain just wanders, and I follow it — it’s more fun that way.

my favourite food

Biryani is my ultimate comfort food, the one thing that can fix my mood faster than any motivational quote ever could. The moment that smell hits the air, I’m already halfway to happiness, imagining myself guarding the pot like it’s a national treasure. I don’t just eat biryani — I experience it, like a full emotional journey from the first spicy bite to the dramatic moment I realise I’ve eaten way more than I planned. There’s just something about that mix of flavours that makes me feel like the universe is briefly in perfect balance
